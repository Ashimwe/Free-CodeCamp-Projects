ASHIMWE Prudent

The Starboy before all starboys

Below is not a tribute page but a beautiful fairytale to be told for milleniums, lived by ASHIMWE Prudent, known by all,appreciated by everyone.

ASHIMWE Prudent Fairytale

  • 1905 -Born in Gatsata,Kigali at September 13 which is now a world-wide holiday
  • 1906 -Became the youngest person to start kindergarten at just 4 months of age
  • 1908 -Joined Gatsata U12 Academy at just 3 years of age
  • 1910 -Joined Gatsata U18 Academy at just 5 years of age
  • 1911 -Lead Gatsata U21 to being the 1910/1911 Rwandan Premier League champions and an Amahoro cup final which Gatsata lost due to Prudent ban because he was not 18 yet
  • 1915 -Joined Arsenal,but was not allowed to play due to being "too young"
  • 1916 -Rallies all around Europe with the saying "Football is for all" claiming the too young rule is stupid that FIFA should let Prudent play
  • 1917 -The too young rule for European leagues is lifted by FIFA and Prudent plays his first senior game for Arsenal at just 12 years of age where he came off the bench and went on to score a hattrick against Manchester United
  • 1918 -Prudent gets a Rwandan national team callup but is banned again because of the too young rule in International competitons
  • 1919 -Rallies all around the world saying the too young rule is stupid
  • 1920 -The too young rule is lifted for all tournaments around the world and Prudent makes his debut for the Rwandan Senior team for the 1922 Coupe D'Afrique
  • 1921 -Prudent comes on in the 92nd minute to score a direct freekick to send Rwanda to their first ever major competiton
  • April 1922 -Prudent leads to an English treble where he went on to be named POTY for the 1921/1922
  • August 1922 -Prudent wins the African cup with Rwanda after coming on for the injured goalkeeper in the 70th minute and went on to make 4 penalty stops
  • September 1922-FIFA invents a trophy for the most outstanding player throughout Europe season,wants it to name it after Prudent but Prudent insisted that it should be named Ballon d'or
  • October 1922 -Prudent wins the first even Ballon D'Or
  • 1923 -Prudent wins the Ballon D'or twice in a row and announces he's taking a one year break from football to study for his doctorate degree
  • 1925 -Prudent leads Rwanda to the World cup and announces his retirement
  • 2005 -Prudent dies of old age
